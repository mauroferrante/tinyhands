Made with ❤️ by a work‑from‑home dad
(and beta‑tested by a tiny chaos goblin).

I'll be deep in a task — finally focused — and then I hear it: the quiet approach. The door opens. A toddler appears. Eyes locked on my keyboard like it's the control panel of a spaceship.

If you've been here, you know the next move: enthusiastic key‑mashing that can definitely ruin your day.

My old "solution" was Photo Booth. Fun for a bit… until it's the same silly face, every day, forever. Also: I'm trying to raise a kid, not a personal‑brand influencer.

So I made Tiny Hands Play: quick little browser games you can pop open fullscreen. No installs, no accounts, no "wait where's the app." Just bookmark it and deploy when tiny hands arrive.

They get colors, sounds, and "I DID IT!" moments. Your work stays safe(ish). You stay in the room. Everyone wins for two minutes.

— A dad who just wanted to finish one thing
